Delta Gamma Iota

Shaping Men of Honor, Purpose, and Brotherhood.

For generations, Delta Gamma Iota has fostered excellence through leadership, academic ambition, resilience, and an unbreakable bond of brotherhood. We are committed to forming men of integrity who uplift their communities and each other.

Our History

Delta Gamma Iota was founded in 1965 at Vincennes University in Vincennes, Indiana. The fraternity was built upon three core principles: Diligence, Brotherhood, and Integrity. In pursuit of growth and advancement, Delta Gamma Iota went national as Sigma Phi Epsilon in 1973.

In 1992, Sigma Phi Epsilon removed all charters from two-year institutions due to revenue restructuring. Rather than let their brotherhood end, the remaining members united with determination and re-founded themselves as Delta Gamma Iota once again on December 5, 1993.

During the Fall of 1997, two Brothers from the Alpha Chapter transferred to Ball State University to continue their education. Inspired to extend the bond they shared in Vincennes, these men began laying the foundation for what would become the Gamma Chapter of Delta Gamma Iota.

With the help of additional Alpha Chapter brothers and newly recruited Founding Fathers, the Gamma Chapter was officially founded on February 7, 1998. Ball State University formally recognized the chapter shortly after, on February 17, 1999.

Since those historic beginnings, many pledge classes have passed through the halls of Delta Gamma Iota. Each Brother carries forward the values of diligence, brotherhood, and integrity — strengthening our legacy and ensuring that future generations continue the tradition.

Today, Delta Gamma Iota proudly celebrates 60 years of excellence, leadership, and lifelong brotherhood.
